This is me testing our creative coding and advanced web animation for fun. For so long, I've been focused front-end work that doesn't challenge my abilities. Since I'm not going to be doing front-end forever, I figured, why not go in while I'm at it?

I've been feeling a bit anxious about my commitment to front-end design. First of all, I never started out as purely a front-end programmer. I began as a designer that used front-end like frameworks such as Framer to protoype advanced features. However, as I began to enter the rabbit hole of code-based prototyping, I entered the field of front-end development and embraced it fully. As most do, I've felt the unnecessary that the front-end community puts on its followers. Medium articles, talks, and twitter threads all encourage front-end programmers to keep up with every new trend, without a discussion on the dangerous stipulations of such an attitude.

The issue with the "I-need-to-know-it-all" attitude isn't ego either. It's fatigue. The front-end community has a interesting way of shaming those who don't follow its new ideas and design patterns. And this isn't to say that theres no validity to new technologies, but often the technologies that they replace aren't outdated in the first place.
